My Process

Making changes within an organization — no matter the size — is hard. The design process I favor helps streamline problem solving.




1. Understand people and the problem space

I focus on sparking conversations with the people involved in a project, learning about the team’s needs and goals, and setting an expectation for open and honest communication. The team immerses themselves in a domain to understand product competitors, technical limitations, and team hypotheses and assumptions. We collect data on end-users.
  • Surveys
  • User interviews
  • Contextual inquiries
  • Competitive/Comparative analysis
  • Heuristic analysis
  • Usability testing

2. Define the opportunity and the plan

We review the assumptions and hypotheses and decide if enough research has been conducted. Through data synthesis, we uncover actionable insights. With teammates and stakeholders, we define the opportunity area and share insights about the end-user. We make a time-bound plan for action, and we set KPIs.
  • Affinity mapping
  • Personas
  • Problem statement
  • User journey
  • Feature prioritization
  • User flows and task flows

3. Create and design solutions

Using insights as a launchpad, we dive into design. Ideas and opportunities are transformed into something tangible. Internal teams iterate and improve upon designs before usability testing begins.
  • Design studios
  • Paper wireframes
  • Mid-fi wireframes
  • Prototypes
  • Site map with navigation study
  • Sync with engineering team

4. Measure success and iterate designs

Thoroughly testing designs and prototypes with the appropriate users is essential. We analyze how successful the UX and UI are, and iterate accordingly.
  • Design studios
  • Usability testing
  • Mid-fi wireframes
  • Prototypes
  • User interviews and surveys

    • 5. Deliver solutions

    After validating designs, we increase fidelity and prepare for development.
  • Prototypes
  • Annotated wireframes
  • Specifications document
  • Setup data storage
  • Paired design and development
